FIA helps accountants and investors analyse overseas investment files, classify FIF exposure, reconcile transactions, calculate income, and produce a review-ready tax report.

What FIA Does

1. File Upload

Upload CSVs, PDFs, broker statements, dividend reports, transaction exports and prior-year workpapers.

2. Data Extraction

Extract holdings, buys, sells, dividends, fees, FX rates, withholding tax and closing balances.

3. Asset Classification

Classify each investment as FIF, exempt, PIE, crypto, foreign pension, Australian exemption or manual review.

4. Reconciliation

Check opening holdings + transactions against closing holdings and flag missing or inconsistent data.

5. FIF Calculation

Calculate FDR, CV, de minimis threshold, foreign tax credits and estimated tax payable.

6. Report Generation

Produce a clean accountant-ready report with assumptions, exceptions, calculation tables and sign-off notes.

Calculation Engine

De Minimis Test

Checks whether total FIF investment cost is under the NZ$50,000 threshold for eligible individuals.

Total FIF cost ≤ NZ$50,000

FDR Method

Calculates FIF income as 5% of opening market value, plus any quick sale adjustment where required.

FIF income = 5% × opening value

CV Method

Calculates actual portfolio movement using closing value, sales, dividends, purchases and opening value.

CV = closing + sales + dividends - purchases - opening

Tax Estimate

Applies the user's marginal tax rate and foreign tax credits to estimate tax payable.

Tax = FIF income × tax rate - credits

Human Sign-Off
